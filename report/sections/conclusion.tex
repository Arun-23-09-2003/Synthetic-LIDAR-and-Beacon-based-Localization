\section{Conclusion}

The repository implements a reproducible MATLAB study of indoor localization with a manifold-aware error-state EKF, synthetic LiDAR pose updates, and intermittent beacon range measurements. The nominal robot state is a 2D NavState matrix, while uncertainty and Kalman updates are carried in a five-dimensional tangent space. Across the three saved scenarios, adaptive beacon covariance improves aggregate beacon-only position RMSE from 0.326 m to 0.286 m, and LiDAR plus adaptive beacon fusion gives the best aggregate position RMSE at 0.0891 m. The strongest conclusion supported by the data is that adaptive beacon weighting is beneficial for the generated beacon-only experiments, while LiDAR pose corrections dominate the final fused localization accuracy in the current simulation setup.
